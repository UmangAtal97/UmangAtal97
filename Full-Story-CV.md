# Umang Atal

## Full Story CV

I am Umang Atal from Pune, Maharashtra. I completed my B.Tech in Mechanical Engineering from V.I.T. Pune in 2019 with a CGPI of 6.05. Thereafter, I did an internship with Tata Motors in the CAPEX Purchase Department at the car plant in Bhosari. During this period, I worked on understanding the techno-commercial aspects of requirements, researching specific needs by collecting information through tools such as SAP, and gathering relevant references that would help inform ongoing purchases. Once the internal research with engineers and software teams was completed, we shortlisted vendors based on quality, feasibility, and cost comparison, and performed payment term analysis before completing the purchase.

My internship concluded in 2019. Around the same time, the recession hit the mechanical market, and the offer I was about to receive from Tata Motors was put on hold and later closed. Thereafter, I completed a 9-month extensive Steam Engineering course from Forbes Marshall. On completion of the course, I was sent to study and create a thesis and later proposals on boiler-related client projects. I was also about to receive a job there, but COVID began, hiring was stopped, and we were called back to Pune.

After that, I worked as a Business Manager for a boiler fuel additive company in Sambhaji Nagar (Aurangabad). During this period, it was found that the parent company was trying to poach clients from us, and as a result, the owner decided to shut down operations. Following that, I worked in a manufacturing company called SM Auto, where I was responsible for project planning and purchasing across three plants in Chakan. I used to travel about 100 km per day. One day, a few goons attacked a supervisor in front of me, and even the security staff could not help. There was also a lack of hygiene at the workplace, and because it was during COVID time, the environment was very difficult. Since my father was a heart patient, I had to leave the job and start searching for new opportunities in IT.

While exploring new career options, I started researching and came across UX. I found it very compelling and felt that it was meant for me. I joined the DesignBoat UX bootcamp, where I learned the foundations of UI/UX design. I started building my portfolio and used to share my learning on LinkedIn. Through this, I received two opportunities: one from Crown Electric in Dubai and another from Enterprise Minds in Pune.

I joined Enterprise Minds in 2021 as an intern. My initial work included creating pitch decks for clients and banners for internal technology workshops in Figma. I was then introduced to one of the clients working with a decentralized protocol system. My role there was to understand requirements with the client, create user flows, get them approved, and then transform them into low-fidelity wireframes and finally high-fidelity mockups. Based on feedback from the client, we followed an agile iteration cycle. One of the most important parts of the role was coordinating with developers, product managers, and QA teams to ensure that the designs were business-feasible and aligned with delivery timelines.

The first project I worked on was the Kochi State Water Department project. We had to design two sides of the application: an admin web app where users could book tickets for water transport and a conductor app where users could verify payments and tickets.

The second project I worked on was a retail consumer-facing mobile application. Another project was a decentralized health protocol where users could book appointments with doctors and diagnostic centers and purchase medicines through the same mobile application. I also worked on a France-based project where users could book tickets to tourist places and also book cabs to reach there on the same platform.

In January 2022, I was offered a permanent role as a UX Engineer at the organization. I continued working on the same client, where I now understood requirements directly from the client, conducted rapid research on user personas, and identified the tasks users perform while using such applications. I created user flows, and once they were approved, it meant that our thought process and the client’s were aligned. After approval, I drafted low-fidelity designs and then high-fidelity mockups, which were handed off to the development team. Once development started, I performed heuristic evaluations and checked screen alignment with the QA team. Once all checks were cleared, we gave the demo to the client. In between this process, my job was to connect with cross-functional teams to understand their pain points and ensure they remained unblocked, while also ensuring that there was not too much deviation from the approved designs and that we delivered business-feasible solutions.

With the same client, we worked on domains such as decentralized data platforms, policy portals for mobility applications, mobility applications for metro, bus, and taxi (for both drivers and riders), online dispute resolution apps for law, Industry 4.0 and manufacturing, smart cities, open street maps for retail, travel and tourism applications such as “A Tryst with Himalayas,” decentralized tourism, and an open agriculture application that helped farmers learn, purchase seeds, fertilizers, and other farm essentials, and also rent farm equipment and get services like labor for crop harvesting. This was implemented for a client from Kenya.

To display all these applications, we created an extremely well-designed Experience Centre where stakeholders could experience the applications and understand how a decentralized network functions and how any business can adopt it easily without hassle.

Recently, we delivered a Unified Energy Protocol that enabled users to buy and rent energy equipment along with energy. I also designed a dashboard for a utility management company to manage electricity using an Agentic AI interface, digitally control the grid, and recommend smart solutions to users to save energy and reduce costs. We also created a decentralized energy protocol and its new experience centre, which was showcased to high-level stakeholders and appreciated.

Over time, we recognized the key elements of the protocol and created a reusable common UI component design library, which helped developers reduce development time and achieve delivery within strict timelines.

The underlying process of designing for this decentralized ecosystem client remained the same. The engagement started in September 2021 and continued until May 2025, during which we delivered a major AI-based project that was demoed at a Stanford round conference.

Alongside this, I was also working with a communications and satellite client, where I was designing an API Policy Management Portal. The process involved product managers giving me the requirements, after which I had to conduct research in the context of user personas and organizational profiles, perform competitive analysis, and deliver high-fidelity designs. I worked in a feedback-based agile product lifecycle and delivered solutions in a short time. Since this was an Enterprise SaaS platform, I identified key components and created a design system that helped speed up the design process.

Meanwhile, Enterprise Minds also started working on a product called Frontend Monitoring System. I contributed by doing UX research, including identifying the target audience, conducting qualitative and quantitative research, analyzing and synthesizing findings, identifying key features, performing competitive analysis, and creating user journeys, user flows, journey maps, and HMWs. Later, the product was successfully deployed for automotive clients and performed well.

Another internal product that I worked on was COSMOS, a cloud cost optimization solution that helped clients identify and track their cloud spend and recommend smart suggestions to optimize and save more. Here, I helped product owners with design systems, research, synthesis, and overall product design.

I also worked on smaller internal requirements such as creating banners, ID cards, and setting up our Research and Development site called AIMS. I designed the spatial experience and logo and assigned superhero identities to all product team members.

I was also assigned to manage the Pune office, where we planned team outings and conducted Friday fun sessions for mental relaxation. I also designed the Enterprise Minds website v1.0. We worked on requirements for a CM dashboard where the Chief Minister could track the progress of ongoing projects related to health, infrastructure, education, law and enforcement, research and development, sports, and more. The dashboard also included metrics to understand public sentiment related to each project, helping officials take better action. A highlight was that the CM could check the live feed to see project progress.

I was involved in usability testing for one of the automotive client projects where the client team claimed that the designs were not performing well. I designed the questionnaire and tasks for users to perform so that we could track the real issue. After testing, we found that our designs were not only easy to understand but also helped the target audience complete tasks in half the time they would have required while using the existing application.

All the projects were completed using Figma, so we designed a proper documentation system to maintain client-wise design files efficiently and structure them in a way that made it easy to create case studies once a project was completed.

In January 2025, we got a new client: one of the famous MMA organizations. I was asked to create responsive applications. One was their fan club website where users could shop for merchandise, book tickets for upcoming events, and stream live fights with exclusive memberships. Another was an app to vote for their favorite athletes to crown them as the People’s Champion. Another was a prediction app for who would win the bouts during live events. Another was an app to read and mint comics of their favorite athletes. Some apps were for flagship events where users were awarded random jackets, and once they scanned their jackets based on the tier level, they could claim rewards. My role was to understand client requirements and come up with design mockups, review them internally with product managers and developers to deliver business-feasible solutions, and create design systems and component libraries for reuse. The process involved understanding client requirements, creating user interfaces and event posters and merchandise using Adobe Photoshop, Illustrator, and Figma.

Recently, Enterprise Minds started working on internal products for workflow automation and creating and managing internal agents. I also contributed to an ERP-based client project by designing AI-backed RBAC modules for recommending the next best action.

I have also designed internship programs for interns and successfully trained and mentored three interns. I also took interviews for the client-side design team.

Apart from this, I am a core committee member of the nation’s largest design community, the Association of Designers of India (ADI), Pune Chapter. I look after government outreach and events. My major contributions included managing and organizing the Pune Design Festival 2026, Design Education Festival 2026, and UX Lighthouse 2026.

Apart from this, I have completed certifications such as the Google UX Design Certificate and IxDF certificates.

I am skilled in UX research, including qualitative and quantitative research, analysis and synthesis, wireframing, prototyping, design systems, UX design, visual design, heuristic evaluation, color theory, typography, brand design, usability testing, cross-functional communication, systems thinking, and design thinking.

The tools I use include Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop, Gemini, Claude Design, Claude Code, Claude Cowork, ChatGPT, Antigravity, Framer, Miro, PowerPoint, Excel, Jira, GitHub, Notion, FigJam, and Figma MCP.
